HotSpots Colorado Features

Location. Location. Location. HotSpots combines open data from the State of Colorado, commercial data, and crowdsourced data to provide Colorado's entrepreneurs with the information they need to select the optimal site for their new or relocating business.

Demographics

Demographic are critical for evaluating who lives in a particular area. The age, gender, education, and income of a community is critical for determining if your business will succeed in this community.

Commercial Landscape

Use HotSpots to identify local competitors, business customer, and services. Find the closest coffee shops, banks, brew pubs, and grocery stores. Coming June 2014!

Zoning

Nearby construction can impair parking, foot traffic, and visibility. Finding out a potential neighbor has permits for a major construction project, can save you the inconvenience and hassle of launching your business in a construction zone. Learn about Denver's zoning code.

Communication Infrastructure

Poor mobile phone reception or slow broadband service can frustrate customers and employees alike.

Location-Based Incentives

Colorado offers various tax incentives and funding programs focused on supporting economic development activities in Colorado. Many of these programs are location based.

Transportation Infrastructure

Your customers and employees rely on parking, public transportation, and bike routes to reach you--know what available. WalkScore data (coming soon) can help you estimate foot traffic and parking needs.

Construction Permits

Knowing whether a prospective location is zoned for your business can you time and money by avoiding the cost and delay of obtaining a zoning variance.

Armed with this data, Colorado's entrepreneurs can quickly identity the optimal HotSpots for their business.
